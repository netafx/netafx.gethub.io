Frequently asked questions

We facilitate the trading of SIPP funds in derivative products (subject to any SIPP scheme restrictions), however, it is important to remember that trading derivatives carries a high level of risk to your capital as it is possible to incur losses that can exceed your initial investment.

When trading derivatives through your SIPP, we restrict dealing or trading services in certain products (such as FX options) and apply increased margin requirements which are higher than standard amounts – thereby reducing the amount of leverage or gearing available to you.

While we do not act as a provider, administrator or trustee of personal pension schemes, we do offer the facility for SIPP members (or their appointed investment advisor) to trade funds held within a SIPP on our trading platforms and thereby, subject to the terms and conditions of their SIPP trustee, access our complete range of trading services.

Saxo offers the facility for SIPP members, or their appointed advisors, to trade funds held within a SIPP through our award-winning platforms. Saxo itself does not function as a trustee. Our full list of approved trustees is available here.

It’s free to open a Saxo SIPP account and there are no hidden charges.

Transferring an existing SIPP to Saxo is simple, just request and complete a Transfer Discharge Form from your current provider.

Once you’ve completed this, email it to us at privatesalesuk@saxomarkets.com, or post it to Saxo Capital Markets UK Limited, 26th Floor, 40 Bank Street, Canary Wharf, London, E14 5DA.

You can open and fund a SIPP in 18 different currencies. Contact our expert sales team if you’d like to know more.

If you didn’t find what you were after, contact us directly and we’ll be pleased to help.
